00 Background

Pillminder is one of my personal passion projects, and it was inspired from watching my grandma struggle taking pills that help fight her epilepsy. It is an app designed to help the elderly remember to take their pills on a daily basis. About 40% of medication-related deaths are related to poor adherence, and pillminder will ensure this number decreases.

01 Problem Statement

Older adults (60+) struggle with memory loss, which causes them to not follow medication plans. They need a mobile application that will help remind them, on a daily basis, to take a pill that will give them a 99% chance of survival from a disease.

02 Literature Review

To tackle such a diverse problem that affects the lives of millions, extensive research was completed. Knowing this, we completed a literature review with 16 sources, 4 research articles, and 12 news articles.



Some of the key takeways were:

  1. People age 65 and older make up 12% of the U.S. population, but account for 34% of all prescription medication use and 30% of all over-the-counter medication use.
  2. Older adults rely on medication lists, pill boxes, family/friends, nurses, or staff to remember when to take pills.
  3. The average elderly patient is taking more than five prescription medications; the average nursing home patient is taking seven medications.
  4. Impaired memory, hearing, and vision loss can make it more difficult to understand and remember medicine instructions.
